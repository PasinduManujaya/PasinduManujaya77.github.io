Individual Recipe Jack Fruit Curry Aluwa Wade

Ingredients with quantities

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) chicken pieces, cleaned and cut into desired sizes
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, sliced (adjust according to spice preference)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4-5 cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2 teaspoons fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder (adjust according to spice preference)
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

Cooking Instructions

1.In a large pan or pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.

2.Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.

3.Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the aromatics release their flavors.

4.Add the cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, cloves, fennel seeds, and cumin seeds. Sauté for another minute to toast the spices.

5.Add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, and chili powder. Mix well to coat the spices evenly.

6.Add the chicken pieces to the pot and brown them on all sides.

7.Once the chicken is browned, add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and the oil starts to separate from the spice mixture.

8.Pour in the coconut milk and stir well. Bring the curry to a simmer.

9.Cover the pot and let the curry cook over low to medium heat for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Stir occasionally.

10.Adjust the salt and spice levels according to your taste.

11.Once the chicken is cooked, remove the curry from heat.
